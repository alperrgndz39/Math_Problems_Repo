# Task 1 — Coin Tossing

Since the order of outcomes matters, each elementary outcome is a **sequence** of heads (H) and tails (T).

---

## 1. Sample space for one coin toss

For one toss, the possible outcomes are:

\[
\Omega_1 = \{H, T\}
\]

### Number of elementary outcomes

\[
|\Omega_1| = 2
\]

### Meaning of an elementary outcome

An elementary outcome represents the result of **one single toss**:

- \(H\): heads
- \(T\): tails

---

## 2. Sample space for two coin tosses

For two tosses, we list all ordered pairs of outcomes:

\[
\Omega_2 = \{HH, HT, TH, TT\}
\]

### Number of elementary outcomes

\[
|\Omega_2| = 4
\]

### Meaning of an elementary outcome

An elementary outcome represents the results of **two tosses in order**.

For example:

- \(HT\): first toss is heads, second toss is tails
- \(TH\): first toss is tails, second toss is heads

These are different because order matters.

---

## 3. Sample space for three coin tosses

For three tosses, we list all ordered triples:

\[
\Omega_3 = \{HHH, HHT, HTH, HTT, THH, THT, TTH, TTT\}
\]

### Number of elementary outcomes

\[
|\Omega_3| = 8
\]

### Meaning of an elementary outcome

An elementary outcome represents the results of **three tosses in order**.

For example:

- \(HHT\): heads on the first two tosses, tails on the third
- \(TTH\): tails on the first two tosses, heads on the third

---

## 4. Summary of the numbers of outcomes

- For one toss:

\[
|\Omega_1| = 2
\]

- For two tosses:

\[
|\Omega_2| = 4
\]

- For three tosses:

\[
|\Omega_3| = 8
\]

In general, for \(n\) coin tosses, the number of elementary outcomes is:

\[
2^n
\]

because each toss has 2 possible outcomes.

---
## Final remark

An **elementary outcome** is one complete, exact description of the experiment.

- For 1 toss: one symbol
- For 2 tosses: one ordered pair
- For 3 tosses: one ordered triple

So the sample space contains all possible ordered sequences of coin results.
