# Ex 1

Solve

$$
\begin{aligned}
2x+3y &= 5\\
x-4y &= -2
\end{aligned}
$$

## Gaussian Elimination

Augmented matrix:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
2 & 3 & | & 5\\
1 & -4 & | & -2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Swap rows:

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -4 & | & -2\\
2 & 3 & | & 5
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Row operation

$$
R_2 = R_2 - 2R_1
$$

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & -4 & | & -2\\
0 & 11 & | & 9
\end{bmatrix}
$$

$$
y=\frac{9}{11}
$$

Substitute into first equation

$$
x -4\left(\frac{9}{11}\right)=-2
$$

$$
x=\frac{14}{11}
$$

**Solution**

$$
x=\frac{14}{11}, \qquad y=\frac{9}{11}
$$

# Ex 2

$$
\begin{aligned}
x+y+z &=6\\
2x-y+3z &=14\\
-x+2y-z &=-2
\end{aligned}
$$

Augmented matrix

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1&1&1&|&6\\
2&-1&3&|&14\\
-1&2&-1&|&-2
\end{bmatrix}
$$

After elimination

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1&1&1&|&6\\
0&-3&1&|&2\\
0&3&0&|&4
\end{bmatrix}
$$

Solve:

$$
y=\frac{4}{3}
$$

Substitute back

$$
x=-\frac{4}{3}, \qquad z=6
$$

**Solution**

$$
(x,y,z)=\left(-\frac{4}{3},\frac{4}{3},6\right)
$$

# Ex 3

Parametric system

$$
\begin{aligned}
x+\lambda y &=1\\
2x+(1+\lambda)y &=3
\end{aligned}
$$

Coefficient matrix

$$
A=
\begin{pmatrix}
1 & \lambda\\
2 & 1+\lambda
\end{pmatrix}
$$

Determinant

$$
\det(A)=1(1+\lambda)-2\lambda
$$

$$
\det(A)=1-\lambda
$$

### Case 1

If

$$
\lambda \ne 1
$$

the system has **one unique solution**.

### Case 2

If

$$
\lambda =1
$$

the system becomes

$$
x+y=1
$$

$$
x+y=\frac{3}{2}
$$

Contradiction → **no solution**.

| λ | Result |
|---|---|
| λ ≠ 1 | unique solution |
| λ = 1 | no solution |

# Ex 4

$$

\begin{pmatrix}
1 & 1 & 1\\

2 & -1 & 3
\end{pmatrix}

$$
b=
\begin{pmatrix}
4\\
1\\
3
\end{pmatrix}

$$
x_1=-7,\quad x_2=4,\quad x_3=7
$$

Check:

1st equation

$$
-7+4+7=4
$$

2nd equation

$$
2(4)-7=1
$$

3rd equation

$$
2(-7)-4+21=3
$$

✔ Correct.
